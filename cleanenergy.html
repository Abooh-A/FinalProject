CLEAN ENERGY

Goal 7 is one of the many goals under the United Nations (UN) sustainable development goals. Its main purpose is to ensure universal access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy by 2030. By doing so, it will lead to ensuring better and safer developments of agriculture, business, communications, education, healthcare and transportation for all. This goal can be accomplished in part by switching the sources of power from fossil fuel to more environmentally friendly and renewable resources such as solar, wind or hydro energy.

SOLAR

This is a great source of renewable energy as it is relatively cheap to install, it is clean meaning it does not create any carbon emission, and its power source (the sun) will be accessible for at least the next 10 billion years. It can work in different climates including places with high snowfall as the sun can reflect off the snow to increase the amount of light hitting the solar panels.

Solar energy in its simplest term is energy generated from the radiated heat and light from the sun. It is one of the easiest renewable energy to install, mainly due to its size. It can be installed to the roofs of buildings and its portable panels can be set up almost anywhere.

WIND

Wind as a source of energy is not a new concept and has been used historically especially on boats and ships. In its simplest term, wind energy is the process in which wind is used to generate mechanical power or electricity. Wind turbines are what allow wind to be tuned into electricity. Wind turns the propeller like blades of a turbine around a rotor which spins a generator to effectively create electricity.

The benefits of wind energy are that it is a clean source of renewable energy, whole communities can be powered by few wind turbines and wind can be harnessed almost everywhere. Wind energy does not rely on water in order to be produced unlike some fossil fuel sources and as such produces no carbon emission. It is one of the most environmentally friendly sources of renewable energy as its emissions and consumption of resources are limited to its production, transportation and installation

HYDRO

Lastly, hydro energy, hydropower or hydroelectricity, is the process of using moving water as a source of energy to generate mechanical power or electricity. It creates power by using diversion structures (most commonly dams) to alter the flow or bodies of water to convert kinetic energy to usable power. No water is reduced during this process.

Hydroelectricity is a great source of renewable energy and has been used since the 18th century to produce power and supplies about 15% of the worldâ€™s electricity. It is reliable as it can meet electricity demands easily, and in the long run it is relatively inexpensive and easy to access. Hydroenergy can also be used for agricultural means through irrigation, especially in areas where droughts are common.
