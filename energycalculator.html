ENERGY CALCULATOR

How to use the carbon calculator

Using the carbon calculator is simple and helps you understand how much carbon dioxide (CO₂) your household appliances produce. Start by entering the appliance name, the power rating, hours of use per day, and the number of appliances. Then enter the emission factor and click Calculate CO₂ to see your results.

Benefits

The calculator helps you make smarter choices by showing which appliances create the most emissions. It supports Sustainable Development Goal 7 by encouraging efficient energy use and helping you reduce your carbon footprint at home.

How to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint in Daily Life

Supporting United Nations Sustainabbe Development Goals 7: Affordable and Clean Energy

You can save electricity by plugging appliances into a power strip and turning them off completely when not in use, including your computer. You can also bike, walk or take public transport to reduce carbon emissions.

Our homes use a wide range of appliances every day, and many of them consume more energy than we realise. By learning a few simple energy-saving habits, we can reduce our electricity use and lower our carbon footprint. These small changes not only help save money on utility bills but also support the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 7, which promotes affordable and clean energy for everyone. With smarter choices, we can make our homes more efficient and help create a more sustainable future.
